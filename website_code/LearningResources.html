Rules Governing the Use of the Library and Resource Centre

Introduction

All students and staff of the College are entitled to register as members of the Library and Resource Centre and to use the facilities for the purpose of their course of study or College employment.

Membership may also be extended to other individuals and to persons nominated from other organisations: a fee is usually charged. Access to the Library and Resource Centre for study purposes may be made available to non-members by arrangement. Details are available in the External Services leaflet.

Use of facilities implies acceptance of the Library and Resource Centre rules printed below. These are designed to protect the interests of all users and ensure the College operates efficiently and effectively. The rules apply in principle to all sites, including workstation rooms and site libraries, notwithstanding some variations in practice in site libraries.

Admission to the Library and Resource Centre is conditional upon observation of the rules, and the staff are empowered to enforce them. Breaches of the rules may be dealt with by specific Library and Resource Centre disciplinary action, including the withdrawal of access, and if appropriate, by invoking the Collegeâ€™s Regulations for Students or staff procedures.

Rules

A. Access

  • The use of Library and Resource Centre facilities is normally limited to use for educational and other College purposes.
  • All individuals wishing to become members must comply with registration procedures, including agreeing to the rules.
  • Non-members may be admitted for study purposes by arrangement. Visitors must sign the Visitors' Book and may need to show identification.
  • ID cards are not transferable. The cardholder is responsible for all borrowed items and their replacement if lost or damaged.
  • Users must show ID cards or identification upon request by College staff.

B. Use of Facilities

  • The Library is not responsible for the loss of or damage to personal belongings. Items left unattended may be removed but returned upon request.
  • All users must maintain quiet. Conversation should be minimal, and none is allowed in 'Silent' areas.
  • Personal stereos are not permitted in the Library.
  • Mobile phones must be turned off in study areas but are allowed in enclosed stairwells.
  • Personal equipment may only use mains electricity if certified for Health & Safety compliance, with written proof.
  • Eating and drinking are not allowed in public areas of the Library.
  • Misuse of furniture, facilities, or materials may result in penalties, including charges for damages.
